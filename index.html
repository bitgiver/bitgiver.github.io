This is BitGiver. A blockchain developed program that will revolutionize charitable giving.

BitGiver ensures that charities, non-profits, and other 501(c)(3) entities are receiving the maximum amount of their donations.

The current payments system is structured such that nearly any donation given via credit card takes 3% on every transaction. According to Giving USA, Americans gave $358.38 billion to charities across the world in 2014. Even if we assume that merely 10% of all those donations occurred via credit cards, that is over $1 billion dollars in transaction fees that did not end up in the hands of those in need. Instead, it ended up with the credit card companies. According to the United Nations World Food Programme, this amount could have fed 5.9 million children per year.

BitGiver reduces transaction costs and maximizes the value we can give to the world. Peer-to-peer payments and distributed ledger systems allow for an efficient, transparent, and effective means of giving.

How it Works

BitGiver locks in the USD:Bitcoin exchange rate for 15-minutes from the moment you begin. Your donation is then sent instantaneously over the blockchain and deposited in the BitGiver wallet, which is held at CoinBase - the largest bitcoin wallet service in the world. After the initial 15-minute window, a new exchange rate will be posted. Each charity's donations are then summed at 11:59pm each night to allow for a one-time bulk donation.

Charitable donations are tax exempt in the United States. Because BitGiver locks in an exchange rate, we prepare an invoice for you that can be submitted for these purposes. Make sure to give your email address, and the payment information will be processed and delivered right to your inbox.
