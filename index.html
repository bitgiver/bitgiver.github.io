This is BitGiver:
A blockchain developed program that will revolutionize charitable giving.

BitGiver ensures that charities, non-profits, education facilities, and other 501(c)(3) entities are receiving the maximum amount of their donations.

The current payments system is structured such that nearly any donation given via credit card charges a 3% fee to merchants on every transaction. According to Giving USA, Americans gave $358.38 billion to charities across the world in 2014. Even if we assume that merely 10% of those donations occurred via credit cards, that is over $1 billion dollars that did not end up in the hands of those in need due to credit card transaction fees . According to the United Nations World Food Programme, this amount could have fed 5.9 million children per year.

BitGiver reduces transaction costs and maximizes the value we can give to the world. Peer-to-peer payments and distributed ledger systems allow for an efficient, transparent, and effective means of giving.

How it Works

BitGiver locks in the USD:Bitcoin exchange rate for 15-minutes from the moment you select your charity. Your donation is then sent instantaneously over the blockchain and deposited in the BitGiver wallet, which is held at CoinBase - the largest bitcoin wallet service in the world. After the initial 15-minute window, a new exchange rate will be posted. Each charity's donations are then automatically forwarded to the corresponding charity.

Tax Benefits

Charitable donations are tax exempt in the United States and in many other countries around the world. Because BitGiver locks in an exchange rate, we prepare a US Dollar invoice for you that can be submitted to receive your full write-off benefits. Make sure to provide your email address when checking out and the payment information will be delivered right to your inbox.

Partnerships

BitGiver partners with charities and other public good organizations to ensure that no money is lost to the hands of middle men. BitGiver does not charge any transaction fees.

What is Bitcoin?
FAQ

Bitcoin is a completely digital money. It is the first decentralized peer-to-peer payment network that is powered by its users with no central authority or middlemen. From a user perspective, Bitcoin is pretty much like cash for the Internet.

How Does Bitcoin Work?
FAQ

Bitcoin is nothing more than a mobile app or computer program that provides a personal Bitcoin wallet and allows a user to send and receive bitcoins with them. This is how Bitcoin works for most users.

What is a Blockchain?
FAQ

A blockchain is an append-only database of transactions which has two key elements: 1) a shared, replicated ledger, and 2) a “consensus algorithm”. In a blockchain, each transaction must reference a balance received from a previous transaction and must be cryptographically signed by the legal approver of the transaction. The linked transactions form an exact chain of title over time.

What is a distributed ledger?
FAQ

A distributed ledger is a digital record of who-owns-what. Unlike traditional database technology, there is no central administrator of the ledger, nor is there a central data store. A blockchain is just one type of distributed ledger - not all distributed ledgers necessarily employ blocks or chain transactions.
