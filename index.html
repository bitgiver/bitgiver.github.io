This is BitGiver. A blockchain developed program that will revolutionize charitable giving.

BitGiver utilizes blockchain technology to ensure that charities, non-profits, and other 501(c)(3) entities are receiving the maximum amount of their donations.

The current payments system is structured such that any donation given via credit card (Visa, Mastercard, American Express) takes 3% on every transaction. In 2014, Americans gave $358.38 billion to charities across the world - even if we assume that merely 10% of those transactions occurred via credit cards, that is over $1 billion dollars that did not end up in the hands of those in need. According to the United Nations World Food Programme, this amount could feed 5.9 million children per year. BitGiver cuts out the middle-man and maximizes the value we can give to the world.

Peer-to-peer payments and distributed ledger systems allow for an efficient, transparent, and effective means of giving.

How it Works

BitGiver locks in the USD:Bitcoin exchange rate for 15-minutes from the moment that you click "donate" - Your donation is then sent instantaneously over the blockchain and deposited in the BitGiver wallet, which is held at CoinBase - the largest bitcoin wallet service in the world. After the initial 15-minute window, a new exchange rate will be posted.

Each donation is then summed according to charity chosen at midnight each night, at which point all funds are deposited at the corresponding account. No charge. No hassle.
